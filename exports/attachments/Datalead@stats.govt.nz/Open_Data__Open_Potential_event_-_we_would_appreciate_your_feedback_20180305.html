Kia ora

 

Thank you for participating in our Open Data, Open Potential event on Friday.

 

It was fantastic to see so many people from the open data community, government, and business gather to hear the Statistics Minister’s aspirations for open data; understand how Stats NZ will support the implementation of the Open Data Action Plan; learn about the exciting world of data ventures; realise how different people are using data for innovation.

 

The event created a real buzz on Twitter, with #OpenPotential18 trending. In all, we had 350 tweets and retweets, resulting in nearly 10,000 impressions (or views).

 

Event information

Presentations and a list of attendees are available on our event page at https://www.data.govt.nz/community/open-data-open-potential/.

 

On this page you’ll also find a link to the full Open Data Action Plan, as well as our Loomio discussion board. You’re encouraged to share your thoughts on the action plan, how you’ll use it, or how it could be improved. The action plan is a living document, and will be updated regularly.

 

Survey

Please provide your feedback on the event itself by completing this short (less than five minutes), confidential survey. The survey is accessible from most devices and will remain open until 5pm on Monday 12 March. https://www.surveygizmo.com/s3/4233326/Open-Data-Open-Potential-post-event

 

We’ve included a few questions on data.govt.nz – an all-of-government website helping people discover and use open data easily.

 

Find out more

You can find out more about what’s happening in the data leadership space at https://www.stats.govt.nz/about-us/data-leadership/, including our plain English fact sheets.

 

Thanks again for helping to make the Open Data, Open Potential event such a success.
